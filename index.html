Our Speciality

Food is taste of life

Love of beauty is taste. The creation of beauty is art.

The ancestors of our first chef, late Abdur Rahim saab, used to cook for the nawabs and that special recipe was handed down the generations. But when the restaurant opened, biryani was not the most sought-after dish. It only became popular after Independence. Among the stars, Khuswant Singh, Satyajit Ray, Gunter Grass and Uttam Kumar used to love Royal's biryani. Satyajit Ray was fond of the Mutton Chaap, Sourav Ganguly loves the Chicken Biryani and Khushwant Singh is a regular whenever he is in town. Yesteryear actress Tabassum would have it packed and take it for Dilip Kumar!"

Our Special Dishes

Royal chicken biryani

Clients Rating:

Royal’s biryani, for instance, is made without the customary Aloo that you'll find in other Kolkata restaurants. And this is the way the Lucknow Nawabs liked it. And Royal gives you that feel. Bang opposite Nakhoda Masjid on Chitpur Road, with it's bazaars, attar shops and jostling crowds, the restaurant could exist in a slice of 19th century Lucknow. The congested locality in which it is situated deters many from venturing there. Only the die-hard fans continue to take the trouble to patronise it, or those who happen to be in the vicinity.

Our Special Dishes

Royal chicken chaap

Clients Rating:

The speciality of the house is the Royal mutton chap. Beaten pieces of meat are marinated in their own special spices and then shallow fried in oil on an iron tawa. If tongues had ears, one could listen to the majestic aloofness of Darbari Kanada on a plate of Royal's biryani, continuously emphasizing on preserving its pristine culture. Along with Biryani came the famous Chaap, and the jugalbandi Royal played out with this duo became a legend around the city and beyond.

Director's Desk

Mr. Owner
name here

Though Royal does not boast of five-star comforts or variety available in, for example, the Park Street menus, it has its loyalists — and not just for the nostalgia factor. It was the novelty of items like Mutton Chaap that clicked with the people of Kolkata. The Nawabs of Lucknow were fond of Mutton Chaap dish and exactly the same cooking procedure was adopted here. It is made of the “Puth” from the back portion of goat, which is later beaten, then marinated with special spices and saffron. Finally cooked in“Mahi Tawa” over charcoal.
"The food is still prepared in a manner straight out of the kitchen of the Nawabs of Lucknow.”
